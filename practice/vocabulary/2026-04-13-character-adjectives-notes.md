# Character Adjectives Notes

Source vocabulary from the attached exercise about personality adjectives used in work context.

| Word | English definition | Polish translation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| naive | when you do not have much experience of the world and believe things too easily | naiwny |
| sensitive | when you are easily upset by what people say about you | wrażliwy |
| arrogant | when you believe or behave as if you know more or are more important than other people | arogancki |
| self-confident | when you feel sure about yourself and your abilities | pewny siebie |
| passionate | when you like something and have strong feelings about it | pełen pasji |
| ambitious | when you have a strong wish to be successful, powerful or rich | ambitny |
| loyal | when you always support something or someone, even when other people do not | lojalny |
| optimistic | when you always think good things will happen | optymistyczny |
| sensible | when you show good judgement and make practical decisions | rozsądny |
| reckless | when you do something dangerous without thinking carefully about the results | lekkomyślny |

## Quick reminder

- `self-confident` is usually positive.
- `arrogant` is clearly negative.
- `ambitious` is often positive, but it can sound negative in some contexts.
- `sensitive` can describe emotions or reactions to criticism.
- `sensible` is positive and means practical and reasonable.
- `reckless` is negative and suggests careless risk.
