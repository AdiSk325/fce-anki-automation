# Repair Drill – Open Cloze, Key Word Transformation, Gapped Text

## Cel

To krótkie ćwiczenie naprawcze po diagnozie startowej. Celuje dokładnie w trzy obszary, które wyszły najsłabiej:

- dependent prepositions and fixed chunks,
- key word transformation,
- discourse links in gapped text.

## Jak pracować

- Zrób ćwiczenie bez pomocy słownika.
- Odpowiedzi wpisuj bezpośrednio pod każdym zadaniem.
- Po ukończeniu odeślij mi ten plik albo jego treść do sprawdzenia.

## Part A – Open cloze repair

Fill each gap with one word only.

1. Many students are not aware ______ how much they can improve through short daily review.

Twoja odpowiedź: in

2. It is important to focus ______ the logic of the whole sentence, not just one word.

Twoja odpowiedź: on

3. Good revision contributes ______ long-term confidence in the exam.

Twoja odpowiedź: for

4. Some learners become too dependent ______ translation instead of context.

Twoja odpowiedź: on

5. This type of task requires a kind ______ precision that many students underestimate.

Twoja odpowiedź: off

6. Candidates should be sensitive ______ tone as well as content when reading.

Twoja odpowiedź: to

## Part B – Key word transformation repair

Use between two and five words, including the word given.

7. People believe that the course is suitable for independent learners.

THOUGHT

The course IS THOUGHT AS suitable for independent learners.

8. It was not necessary for me to rewrite the whole essay.

NEED

I DON'T NEED TO REWRITING the whole essay.

9. I started learning phrasal verbs three months ago.

FOR

I HAVE LEARNED PHRASAL VERBS FOR three months.

10. I'm sorry that I didn't check my answers more carefully.

WISH

I WISH I HAD CHECKED my answers more carefully.

## Part C – Gapped text repair

Choose the best sentence A-D for each gap. There is one extra sentence.

Many learners think that improvement is only visible when they get a much better score in a test. That belief is understandable, but it can be misleading. Progress often appears first in smaller ways, such as making fewer repeated mistakes or completing a task with less hesitation. __(11)__

Another useful habit is keeping brief notes after each study session. These notes do not need to be long. A few lines about what felt easy, what felt difficult and what should be revised next are often enough. __(12)__

Over time, these small records make learning more visible. Instead of relying on memory or mood, students can look back and notice patterns in their performance. __(13)__

### Sentences

A This helps turn vague impressions into practical next steps.
B As a result, motivation becomes easier to protect.
C In fact, some students avoid reflection because they find it too encouraging.
D That is why it is worth paying attention to partial improvement.

Twoje odpowiedzi:

11. C
12. A
13. B
